Standard fidelity measures used across quantum literature — computed against a known ideal distribution.

Hellinger Fidelity (HF)

$HF(p, q) = \left( \sum_i \sqrt{p_i\, q_i} \right)^{\!2}$

Overlap between experimental and ideal distributions — shape similarity.

Bounded in $[0, 1]$ — higher is better.

Total Variation Distance (TVD)

$TVD(p, q) = \tfrac{1}{2} \sum_i \lvert p_i - q_i \rvert$

Worst-case divergence between distributions — pointwise discrepancy.

Bounded in $[0, 1]$ — lower is better.

Widely used to validate quantum execution quality against a known ideal distribution.
