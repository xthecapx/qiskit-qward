What is Quantum Computing?

Quantum Properties
Superposition A qubit holds a combination of 0 and 1 — data encoded in amplitudes.
Entanglement Non-classical correlation between qubits — joint state cannot be described independently.
Interference Amplitudes combine — constructive amplifies correct answers, destructive cancels wrong ones.
Decoherence Loss of quantum information to the environment — bounds operation time.
Measurement Irreversible readout — projects the qubit onto a classical bit to verify the result.
Beyond Classical
  • Solves problems infeasible for classical systems
  • Cryptography, optimization, molecular simulation
  • Software ecosystem: Qiskit, Q#, Cirq, PennyLane
Substantial investment from governments and industry (Gartner, McKinsey)
